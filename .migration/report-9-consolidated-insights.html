2.6 Key Insights Summary

1. "80/20" Rule Applies: 5 templates (Form, Category Deep, Promotion, Tech Resources, PAS) cover 82% of all pages. Focusing development on these 5 templates first maximizes coverage.

2. 10 blocks cover 90%+ of needs: hero, cards, columns, teaser, form, sidebar-nav, section-metadata, accordion, anchor-nav, and mondrian appear across nearly all templates. Developing these 10 blocks first unlocks migration of 90%+ of pages.

3. Legacy platform adds 1.5-2x complexity: Templates T03, T09, T11, T12, T14, T18, T20, T21 include Legacy platform pages that use different DOM structures (parsys, cq-colctrl, wells). Each block needs platform-aware parsers in the import infrastructure.

4. "Mixed" templates are the hardest: T03 (16,758 pages) and T05 (18,521 pages) contain pages on BOTH Modern and Legacy platforms. These require dual-parser import infrastructure and represent the highest import complexity.

5. Template consolidation reduces EDS templates by 43%: 21 AEM templates can map to ~12 EDS templates through strategic merging of similar structures. This reduces maintenance overhead and authoring complexity.

6. 8 blocks can be deferred to Phase 2: chapter-toc, handbook-search, video-playlist, workflow-stepper, video-teaser, video-modal, csr-pillars, and pipeline affect only ~1,100 pages combined. Deferring these saves ~20 dev days.
